Event No. 1

Day and Date: Saturday, 25th Feb 2017
Time: 10:30 AM onwards
Venue: IBM Lab (301), Computer Department, ZES's Zeal College of Engineering and Research, Narhe, Pune

Mozilla Club Zeal got off to a great start in the year 2017. After carefully picking the right candidates for Technology Lead, Protect Lead and Teach Lead we started brainstorming about what our first event as Mozilla Campus Club will be. As the club had to conduct activities under all the three domains, we thought why not bring them together and conduct a grand event? This little thought paved the way to our club's first ever event of 2017 as a Mozilla Campus Club.

INTRODUCTIONS

All the pre-event preparations were done and we were ready to get the event underway. Participants started pouring in and the available seats were quickly getting occupied. A bunch of the participants were completely unaware of what happend to Zeal Firefox Club and what the new Mozilla Campus Clubs deal was. We were expecting that and so we decided to give a small intro. Our new Teach Lead, Ms. Snehal Samak, was up to the task and she explained the transition from Firefox Student Ambassadors to Mozilla Campus Clubs. After the introducton of the club, it was time to hit the new leads with the spotlight. Everyone introduced themselves and what are their roles and responsibilities.
Here's a quick overview of all our leads:

  • Club Captain : Dhanesh B. Sabane
  • Club co-Captain : Shubham Yadav
  • Technology Lead: Yash Kulkarni
  • Teach Lead: Snehal Samak
  • Protect Lead: Shubham Priyadarshi
  • Find out more about them

WORKING WITH VERSION CONTROL SYSTEMS - GIT

Moving on we began with the first activity of the day, getting familiar with git - the stupid content tracker.

Wonderful event going on !! #git #python #pyladies #MozClubZeal #WebVRIndia #mozillacampusclub pic.twitter.com/jb11XwRFhp

— Mozilla Club Zeal (@MozClubZeal) February 25, 2017

Our Club Captain, Dhanesh, introduced the basic concepts of version control systems and explained how we can use them to make our life easier and our work productive. Then, he directly began with the hands-on session and walked through all the important git commands like: 

git init
git add
git commit
git push
git pull
git fetch
git diff
git show

People tried their hands at pushing and pulling from their remote GitHub directory and they loved it!

ONLINE PETITIONS - A WAY TO FIGHT FOR AN OPEN WEB

After a quick lunch break, all the participants were refreshed for the upcoming sessions. Our new lead, Shubham and his compatriot, Prinkle, started with their session. They explained what online petitions are and how they can be instrumental in #ProtectingtheWeb. They demonstrated a successful online petition and asked people to sign the ongoing petition against #InternetShutdowns in India.

INTRODUCTION TO WEBVR (A-FRAME)

It was now time to get back to some coding. Dhanesh again stepped up for this one and showed an intriguing presentation about Virtual Reality apps and how everyone can get started with only a browser and a text editor.

Understanding WebVR ! #WebVRIndia pic.twitter.com/mxQHsY2N73

— Mozilla Club Zeal (@MozClubZeal) February 25, 2017

All the participants fired up their text editors and coded their way to their first Hello World program using A-Frame.

Helloworld WebVR ! #WebVRIndia pic.twitter.com/JcJyexjzlY

— Mozilla Club Zeal (@MozClubZeal) February 25, 2017

A few participants went out of the way and designed some more complex apps with rotating shapes and a few more effects. The quick output that they got by just a page refresh on their browser made this task even more interesting.

GAME DEVELOPMENT IN PYTHON

The clock was ticking fast and we had already reached the last session of the day. Shubham who had a solid experience in developing basic apps using the Arcade library was the speaker for this session. Our pre-event research concluded that a majority of the participants were completely new to Python. Hence, Shubham first started with some basic concepts of Python and eased the participants into it. After completing the introductory session, he moved on to explaining the arcade library and helped the participants develop a basic game.

Using #Python #arcade library for game development by @yadavs4shubham #MozillaCampusClubs @MozClubZeal @ZealEduSoc pic.twitter.com/YXWtiC1IbZ

— Mozilla Club Zeal (@MozClubZeal) February 25, 2017

By the end of the day, the total coding time was 5 hours! We asked the participants to give us some feedback on the day and all of them loved it! The event concluded on a positive note. We even declared a special prize to the participant who develops an awesome WebVR app/game and submits it to the club.

If you are a student of ZCOER and would love to take up this challenge, develop an awesome WebVR app and submit it to us by sending an email to mcz@zealeducation.com.

Take a look at aframe.io to get all the resources you need to get started with your first WebVR app.
